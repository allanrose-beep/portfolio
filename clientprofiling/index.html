Client Profiling

Role: Product Manager

Company: Crimson Interactive

Brands Involved: Enago, Publication Support, Ulatus (PS)

Impact: 18% Improvement in Client conversion rate.

Ideation to execution

The Problem We Couldn’t Ignore

We noticed something odd: conversion rates from inquiry to confirmation were dropping steadily, and across geographies.
Initially, it looked like just a market blip. But when we dug deeper, it became clear:
We weren’t losing leads, we were leaking them.

The Investigation

We pulled data from CRM logs, listened in on CS calls, and sat down with the CS reps. Turns out, each CS agent was going in blind, with no memory of the client's earlier interactions, service preferences, or previous follow-ups. Every call felt like starting from scratch. And in a world of impatient users, that was costing us.

"I called, but I didn’t know what they wanted."

"She sounded annoyed, like I should’ve known she asked for Academic Editing last week."

"They keep asking ‘Don’t you already have my info?’"

The Core Insight

Our customer service team was working hard, but they lacked the critical information needed to make meaningful connections with potential clients. Without visibility into previous interactions, they couldn't demonstrate that we valued the relationship.

This gap created a jarring experience for customers who expected continuity but instead encountered what felt like amnesia with each new interaction..

“

We don’t need more outreach, we need smarter outreach.
– Product Manager, Enago

Solution: From Conversion Drop to Context Engine

The goal was simple, but critical: Boost lead conversion by giving the CS team everything they need to personalize interactions — on a single screen.

  • Enable tailored conversations across the user journey
  • Power more effective upselling and cross-selling
  • Increase user retention and satisfaction through personalization
  • Reduce back-and-forth and frustration from repeated cold outreach

How we Built it: From Sheets to System

So we built a proper system, a lightweight internal tool that became the team’s go-to command center.

Here’s what we focused on:

  • A clean, scrollable view per lead
  • All info in one place: service requested, location, form data
  • Outcome tagging
  • Optional notes to log edge cases (language barrier, urgency, etc.)
    These notes would later on be used as data to create more logic and make this Tool smarter

Raw Idea

Before we had a design, we had a list.

We started with a basic Excel sheet to centralize user data, just enough to test whether consolidating this information made CS work easier.

Lo-fi Wireframe

Once we validated the need, we mocked up a wireframe screen in Figma, focusing purely on layout and data groupings.

Finalized version

Final Thought

This dashboard began as a simple Excel sheet triggered by one insight:

By layering context into a single screen, we turned guesswork into confident, personalized conversations and improved conversions in the process.

Great internal tools don’t just save time, they sharpen human judgment.
