IPL Statistics Dashboard

Role: Business Analyst

Dashboard

Project Overview

In 2023, while completing my Business Analytics program with UpGrad, I got the opportunity to work on a semi-final project that turned out to be one of the most rewarding experiences of the course. The brief was simple on paper but layered in execution:
Build a fully interactive dashboard to visualize IPL statistics.

The Project Brief

I was given two datasets:

  • One with match-level insights
  • Another with ball-by-ball delivery details

My goal was to combine these datasets and build a comprehensive Tableau dashboard that could serve as a powerful tool for sports journalists, specifically, the Sports Editor and design team at a fictional news agency. They would use it to pull out quick insights, create infographics, and better understand the trends in the IPL from 2008 onwards.

My Approach

I started by thoroughly examining both datasets to understand the structure and identify overlaps. The data was messy, scattered across sheets and riddled with inconsistencies. So, the first major task was cleaning, merging, and organizing the data using Excel. Once I had a clean base, I looked at the headers and started noting down the parts I wanted to include on the dashboard.

Before jumping into Tableau, I took a low-tech approach, I grabbed a piece of paper and sketched out how I wanted the dashboard to look. This wireframe helped me visualize the flow and determine what filters and visual elements would add the most value.

Once I was clear on the layout, I started building the dashboard in Tableau, one chart at a time. Eventually, I stitched everything together into a single interactive dashboard.

What I Built

  • Season Filter Panel: A vertical panel with clickable buttons to view data season-wise from 2008 to 2017.
  • Top Performers at a Glance: A section showcasing the highest team totals, Orange Cap (most runs), and Purple Cap (most wickets) players.
  • Toss vs Match Win Analysis: A donut chart showing how often the toss winner ended up winning the match, categorized by whether they chose to bat or field.
  • Dismissal Breakdown: A pie chart revealing how players typically got out, from catches to LBWs.
  • Average Runs Per Match: A bar chart comparing teams based on their average match scores.
  • Most Played Venues: A geographical heatmap showing which stadiums hosted the most IPL games.

Technical Implementation & Challenges Addressed

Data Preparation:
I cleaned and structured the fragmented dataset using Excel, transforming it into an analysis-ready format.

Data Visualization:
I created interactive dashboards in Tableau using various charts (bar, pie, donut, and heatmaps) and optimized performance for large datasets through aggregation and other techniques.

User Interactivity:
To enhance user experience, I integrated parameter controls, calculated fields, and action filters, along with clear legends and tooltips for dynamic and easy data exploration.
